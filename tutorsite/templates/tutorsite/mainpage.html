Who is Francis?

I have been a guitarist since the age of ten and a performing musician since sixteen. I learned to play primarily through the use of improvisation. This gives me a unique appreciation for the value of alternative teaching methods.

I have learned to contend with the challenges presented to me by my attention deficit brain and I am more than happy to share my experience.

My approach & Who will benefit

My style of teaching works best for those who aren't suited to a cold academic approach, and who would rather enjoy the learning process than attempt to struggle through it as quickly as possible. My main goal is to eliminate guilt and inferiority from the landscape of learning, which is why I don't take students through grades.

My style of learning was adapted to best suit my own ADHD growing up and is consequently a good deal more entertaining than traditional methods. I find very few people pick up the guitar because they really want an extra weekly chore. Thus I use improvisation as a primary tool for practice and though I may set homework, I do not oblige my students to spend their time worrying about it. The guitar must not become associated early on with chores and hard work because once you reach a basic competency it is neither of these things.
